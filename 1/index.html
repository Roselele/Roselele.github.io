When taking a close-up selfie, the distance between camera and nose is much shorter compared to the distance between camera and side of the face, causing a significant distorting effect. When taking the selfie faraway, that distance difference becomes relatively small, and the distortion is gone.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

When stepping back and zooming in, the relative distance difference between objects and camera becomes smaller, so the perspective is weaker. The space looks "compressed". We can also understand this through projection. When we step back, the objects in the previous photo are projecting to a smaller area of camera space, and the diffenrence of their sizes natrually becomes smaller.

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom

Dolly zoom uses the space compression effect mensioned above to create an illusion that the background objects are rushing towards the camera.
